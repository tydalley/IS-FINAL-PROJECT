How to Do a Wakesurf 360

Landing a wakesurf 360 takes patience, balance, and repetition. The biggest thing I have learned is that staying calm and centered works better than trying to force the spin too hard.

```
  1. Get comfortable riding in the pocket
    • Make sure you can ride without the rope consistently first.
    • Stay close enough to the strongest part of the wave.
    • Keep your knees bent and your weight centered.
  2. Set up with balance and control
    • Stay low and athletic before starting the spin.
    • Keep your eyes up instead of looking down at the board.
    • Do not rush the trick from a bad position on the board or the wave.
  3. Start the 360 smoothly
    • Use your shoulders and head to begin turning. When starting did your hand in the water as well.
    • Let the board rotate under you rather than forcing it too aggressively.
    • Stay centered over the board as it comes around.
  4. Spot the wave and recover
    • Once you come around, find the wave quickly.
    • Absorb movement with bent knees.
    • Settle back into a stable stance and keep riding.
```

Wakesurf Tips

```

Stay in the Pocket

Most progress comes when you stay where the wave is strongest. If you drift too far back, tricks get much harder.

Keep Your Knees Bent

A low athletic stance helps with balance, recovery, and control. It also makes spins feel more stable.

Do Not Rush the Spin

Smooth rotation usually works better than trying to snap into a 360 too fast. Staying calm helps more than overthinking.

Practice a Little Every Session

Improvement usually comes from consistent attempts over time. Even small improvements each set add up.
```
